Exoplanet Flashcards

What is the major challenge in directly imaging exoplanets?

The stars they orbit are millions of times brighter than the planets, making it hard to detect the planets' light.

What is coronography?

A technique that uses a device inside a telescope to block a star’s light, allowing astronomers to detect exoplanets.

What is a starshade?

A separate spacecraft that blocks a star’s light before it reaches a telescope, helping to image exoplanets.

How does astrometry help in finding exoplanets?

It tracks the wobble of a star caused by a planet’s gravity, observing the star's position changes over time.

What makes astrometry a difficult technique to use?

The star’s wobble is tiny and hard to detect, especially for smaller planets.

How do scientists use reference stars in astrometry?

They compare the position of the target star with nearby reference stars to detect movement caused by exoplanets.

What are Hot Jupiters?

Gas giants that orbit very close to their stars, with high surface temperatures and short orbits.

What are terrestrial exoplanets?

Earth-sized planets composed of rock, silicate, water, or carbon, with solid or liquid surfaces.

What are Super-Earths or Mini-Neptunes?

Planets up to twice the size of Earth with up to 10 times its mass, possibly gas giants or terrestrial planets.

What are Neptunian exoplanets?

Planets similar in size to Neptune and Uranus, with mixed interiors and heavy metal cores.

What are rogue exoplanets?

Planets that are not gravitationally bound to any star and wander the galaxy alone.

What was the first exoplanet discovered around a sun-like star?

51 Pegasi b (Dimidium), discovered in 1995.

What type of exoplanet is 51 Pegasi b?

A Hot Jupiter.

How far is 51 Pegasi b from Earth?

About 50 light-years away.

What is significant about Kepler-22b’s location in its star system?

It was the first exoplanet found in its star’s habitable zone.

How far is Kepler-22b from Earth?

Over 600 light-years away.

What is the estimated surface temperature of Kepler-22b?

72°F (22°C).

Which exoplanet is the closest known to Earth?

Proxima Centauri b.

How far away is Proxima Centauri b from Earth?

Just over 4 light-years away.

Which exoplanet is believed to have oceans of water beneath a thick ice shell?

GJ 1214 b.

What exoplanet is known for being an ultrahot Jupiter with incredibly high winds?

KELT-9b.

What is KELT-9b's surface temperature?

Around 7,800°F (4,300°C).

Which system has the most planets discovered?

The TRAPPIST-1 system, with 7 planets.

What is the habitable zone?

The region around a star where conditions might allow liquid water to exist on a planet’s surface.

What is the habitable zone also known as?

The "Goldilocks zone."

What is unique about the habitable zones around red dwarf stars?

They are much closer to the star because red dwarfs emit less heat than larger stars.

What affects the size of a star’s habitable zone?

The size and temperature of the star.

What gases are typically found in the atmospheres of exoplanets?

Hydrogen, helium, methane, and carbon dioxide.

Why is the study of exoplanet atmospheres important?

It provides clues about the planet’s composition and potential habitability.

What method is often used to study exoplanet atmospheres?

Transit spectroscopy, analyzing the light that passes through the planet’s atmosphere during a transit.

What can scientists learn by studying the color of an exoplanet?

The composition of its atmosphere and surface.

What have been some of the biggest surprises when studying exoplanet atmospheres?

The discovery of super-Earths with thick atmospheres and unexpected chemical compositions.

What was the primary mission of the Kepler Space Telescope?

To discover Earth-like exoplanets in the habitable zones of their stars.

How many exoplanets did the Kepler mission confirm?

Over 2,600 exoplanets.

What is TESS?

The Transiting Exoplanet Survey Satellite, a mission designed to discover exoplanets orbiting nearby stars.

What is the James Webb Space Telescope’s role in exoplanet discovery?

It is expected to study exoplanet atmospheres and search for signs of habitability.
